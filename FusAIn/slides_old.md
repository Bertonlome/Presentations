<section> <h2>What fashion people like (and fear) in FusAIn</h2> <div class="fragment row-flex"> <div class="text-content"> <h3>1. Alignment with existing practice</h3> <ul> <li>Works by <strong>adding, recomposing, layering</strong> materials – like building a moodboard on screen.</li> <li>Lets designers “go fetch a material, drop it, recombine it” – close to <strong>draping, collage, textile sampling</strong>.</li> <li>GenAI becomes a way to <strong>amplify an intention</strong>, not replace it.</li> </ul> </div> <div class="image-stack"> <!-- Placeholder: FusAIn interface screenshot --> <img src="fusain_ui_placeholder.png" alt="Placeholder – FusAIn interface with pens and canvas"> </div> </div> <div class="fragment row-flex" style="margin-top: 1em;"> <div class="text-content"> <h3>2. Ethics & boundaries (according to Louise)</h3> <ul> <li><strong>Authorship:</strong> if the designer keeps control, GenAI feels acceptable (and less risky for copyright).</li> <li><strong>Environmental impact:</strong> heavy compute cost of AI is a major friction point.</li> <li><strong>Personal limit:</strong> use GenAI as an <em>occasional support</em>, not a constant dependency.</li> </ul> </div> <div class="image-stack"> <!-- Placeholder: scales icon craft vs AI --> <img src="craft_ai_balance_placeholder.png" alt="Placeholder – balance between craftsmanship and AI"> </div> </div> <div class="footnotes"> <div class="fn-item"> <span class="fn-num">Louise.</span> <span class="fn-text">Elle parle d’« alliance artisanat/technologie » et d’un outil qui « amplifie l’intention du designer plutôt qu’une technologie qui cherche à la remplacer ».</span> </div> </div> </section>

<section> <h2>Before GenAI (1): Analog tools for generating fashion ideas</h2> <div class="fragment row-flex"> <div class="text-content"> <h3>Early “idea machines” were physical</h3> <ul> <li><strong>Sketchbooks & croquis:</strong> fast iteration, exaggeration of silhouette, proportion, movement.</li> <li><strong>Tracing paper & overlays:</strong> copy a base figure, explore multiple variations (necklines, sleeves, drapes).</li> <li><strong>Moodboards:</strong> magazines, Polaroids, fabric scraps, trims – a manual retrieval engine of references.</li> <li><strong>Draping on dress forms:</strong> 3D “live prototyping” – the mannequin as a physical simulation tool.</li> <li><strong>Photocopiers & collage:</strong> scale, repeat, distort motifs and silhouettes by cutting and re-pasting.</li> </ul> </div> <div class="image-stack"> <!-- Placeholder: analog fashion tools (sketchbook, mannequin, moodboard) --> <img src="analog_fashion_tools_placeholder.png" alt="Placeholder – sketchbook, mannequin, moodboard"> </div> </div> <div class="footnotes"> <div class="fn-item"> <span class="fn-num">Note.</span> <span class="fn-text">All of these tools already support deconstruction / reconstruction of shapes, textures, and references – the same logic FusAIn tries to digitize.</span> </div> </div> </section>

---

<section> <h2>Before GenAI (2): Digital & semi-generative design tools</h2> <div class="fragment row-flex"> <div class="text-content"> <h3>Digitalization of the fashion workflow</h3> <ul> <li><strong>Photoshop / Illustrator:</strong> digital croquis, colorways, print repeats, rapid re-coloring and scaling.</li> <li><strong>CAD for pattern-making:</strong> tools like Lectra, Gerber, Optitex – parametrized blocks, grading rules.</li> <li><strong>3D garment simulation:</strong> CLO3D, Browzwear – virtual drape and fit, fabric libraries, avatar bodies.</li> <li><strong>Trend & material databases:</strong> online libraries (e.g., textile mills, WGSN-like services) as “search engines” for inspiration.</li> </ul> </div> <div class="image-stack"> <!-- Placeholder: screenshot collage of CAD / 3D garment software --> <img src="cad_clo3d_placeholder.png" alt="Placeholder – CAD and 3D garment interfaces"> </div> </div> <div class="fragment row-flex" style="margin-top: 1em;"> <div class="text-content"> <h3>Generative before GenAI</h3> <ul> <li><strong>Parametric tools in product design:</strong> Grasshopper/Rhino used for shoes, accessories, structures.</li> <li><strong>Rule-based pattern systems:</strong> scripts/macros to auto-generate size ranges or detail variants.</li> <li>These systems were already <strong>algorithmic</strong>, but not yet “learning” from data as GenAI does.</li> </ul> </div> <div class="image-stack"> <!-- Placeholder: Grasshopper graph / parametric shoe design --> <img src="parametric_design_placeholder.png" alt="Placeholder – parametric design graph and shoe model"> </div> </div> <div class="footnotes"> <div class="fn-item"> <span class="fn-num">Take-away.</span> <span class="fn-text">FusAIn sits on top of a long lineage: from sketchbook → photocopier → Photoshop → CAD/3D → parametric tools – it continues the same goal of helping designers explore, combine, and refine visual ideas.</span> </div> </div> </section>
